part 1

preparation

The interface I choose is the water filling stations in Wellness. Everyone has access to these as they are seen in Health Services although for my specific personas I focused on the ones in the dorm hallways. In this interface, you can fill water bottles and also drink from one of the two water fountains. You can activate the water to drink by the front or side buttons and the water for filling the water bottle is activated by a sensor.

part 2

recording observations

Watching the user interact with the interface I noticed some key things. Everyone who went to drink water pushed two buttons whether it was the two in the front or the two on the side. Height definitely played a factor in how easy the fountain was to use as those who were taller had difficulty leaning down to drink. Those using the water filter station acted in two manners. A lot kept their hand on their water bottle while it was filling while others stuck it underneath and paced around as it filled. When it came to removing the bottle, the bottle had either overflowed which indicated it needed to be removed or they stopped it with a small but noticeable amount left to fill.

interview summary

Questions:

  • On a scale of 1 - 10 how easy do you find this interface to use?
  • Are there any components that are hard to understand?
  • Do you typically use the front or the side buttons?
  • Do push one or two buttons?
  • How often do you use the water filler?
  • How often do you use the drinking fountain?
    • Summary: The average answers for how easy this interface was to use was a 3. However, only one person commented on a specific component that was hard to understand. This component was the water filler as there is no clear indication of how to stop the water from flowing, only to get started. The reasons for this interface not being the easiest to use was as a child it might be hard to understand at first that each fountain does the same thing and if you cannot read the instructions for the water filler you wouldnâ€™t know that it fills water bottles. As stated in the observation everyone pushed two buttons although only one is need to get water flowing. There was an equal distribution of those who use the side buttons and those who use the front. The use of the interface was split evenly between drinking and filling. Those who have a water bottle only ever used the drinking fountain to fill their water bottle up. They believed they averaged around 4 times a day filling their water bottles. The ones who used it entirely for drinking did not have water bottles. The come to the fountain multiple times a day to quench their thirst. However, if they had a water bottle they think they would stop coming just for a drink and more for filling up their water bottles.
